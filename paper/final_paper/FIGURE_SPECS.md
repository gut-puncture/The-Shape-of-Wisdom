# Figure Specifications — The Shape of Wisdom

Each figure is generated by `sow.v2.figures.paper_figures` from pre-computed
parquet files. No GPU inference is required.

Regenerate all figures:
```bash
python scripts/v2/regenerate_paper_figures.py \
    --parquet-dir results/parquet \
    --output-dir paper/final_paper/figures
```

---

## Figure 1: Three Primitives (`fig1_primitives.pdf`)

**Takeaway**: Three quantities separate transformer decisions into four
qualitatively distinct trajectory types.

| Panel | Metric | Y-axis | Aggregation |
|-------|--------|--------|-------------|
| (a) | `delta` | Decision margin delta (logits) | Mean +/- SEM band (stable types) |
| (b) | `drift` | Per-layer drift g (logits) | Mean, no band |
| (c) | `boundary` | Boundary distance (logits) | Mean +/- SEM band (stable types) |

- **Layout**: 1x3, full-width (6.75 x 2.2 in)
- **X-axis**: Normalised depth l/(L-1)
- **Lines**: 4 trajectory types, each in its TRAJ colour
- **Reference**: panel (c) has horizontal dashed line at b_min = 0.3
- **Data files**: `decision_metrics.parquet` (276,000 rows), `prompt_types.parquet` (9,000 rows)
- **Expected columns**: model_id, prompt_uid, layer_index, delta, drift, boundary, trajectory_type

## Figure 2: Phase Diagram (`fig2_phase_diagram.pdf`)

**Takeaway**: Boundary proximity and tail oscillation form two algebraically
independent axes that cleanly separate trajectory types.

| Axis | Metric | Computation |
|------|--------|-------------|
| X | Boundary at tail entry | abs(delta) at layer L - tau |
| Y | Sign flips in tail | Count of sign changes in delta over last tau = 8 layers |

- **Layout**: 1x1, column-width (3.25 x 2.8 in)
- **Markers**: scatter, s=6, alpha=0.35, jittered vertically +/- 0.12
- **Reference**: vertical dashed at b_min = 0.3, horizontal dashed at flip = 0.5
- **Data files**: `decision_metrics.parquet`, `prompt_types.parquet`
- **Aggregation**: Per (model, prompt) summary

## Figure 3: Drift Decomposition (`fig3_decomposition.pdf`)

**Takeaway**: Attention drives toward the correct answer in mid-layers while
MLP provides the late push; for wrong answers both forces reverse direction.

| Panel | Content | Lines / Fill |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| (a) | Stable-correct decomposition | fill_between(s_attn), fill_between(s_mlp), dashed drift |
| (b) | Stable-wrong decomposition | same layout, mirror pattern |
| (c) | Cumulative contributions (SC) | cumsum(s_attn), cumsum(s_mlp) |
| (d) | Dominance ratio by type | attention fraction, 4 type lines |

- **Layout**: 2x2, full-width (6.75 x 4.0 in)
- **Data files**: `tracing_scalars.parquet` (55,200 rows), `prompt_types.parquet`
- **Expected columns**: model_id, prompt_uid, layer_index, s_attn, s_mlp, drift, trajectory_type
- **Depth binning**: 20 bins (panels a-c), 10 bins (panel d)
- **Annotations**: "Attn steers" on panel (a), dominance labels on panel (d)

## Figure 4: Attention Routing (`fig4_attention_routing.pdf`)

**Takeaway**: Stable-correct trajectories route disproportionate attention
mass to the correct option; stable-wrong trajectories route to the
competitor — a clean sign reversal at mid-depth.

| Panel | Metric | Y-axis |
|-------|--------|--------|
| (a) | Mass difference (correct - competitor) per token | Delta mass |
| (b) | Contribution difference (correct - competitor) | Delta contribution |

- **Layout**: 1x2, full-width (6.75 x 2.5 in)
- **Lines**: 2 per panel — stable_correct (green) and stable_wrong (red)
- **Fill**: fill_between to zero, alpha=0.12
- **Data files**: `attention_mass_by_span.parquet` (386,400 rows), `attention_contrib_by_span.parquet` (386,400 rows), `decision_metrics.parquet`, `prompt_types.parquet`
- **Remapping**: Absolute span labels (option_A) -> relative (option_correct, option_competitor, option_other) using final-layer competitor key

## Figure 5: Counterfactual Validation (`fig5_counterfactuals.pdf`)

**Takeaway**: Substituting attention from successful trajectories shifts 63%
of failing trajectories positive; MLP substitution shifts 82% positive with
a 4x larger mean effect.

| Panel | Content | Plot type |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| (a) | Simulated removal delta_shift by component | Violin |
| (b) | Substitution mean + CI; fraction positive | Lollipop |
| (c) | Span deletion effect distributions by label | Histogram |
| (d) | Negative controls vs observed | Horizontal bar |

- **Layout**: 2x2, full-width (6.75 x 3.8 in)
- **Data files**: `ablation_results.parquet` (3,600 rows), `patching_results.parquet` (1,800 rows), `span_labels.parquet` (4,200 rows), `negative_controls.parquet` (3 rows)
- **Key values**: attention removal mean ~-1.35, MLP removal mean ~-5.40; observed evidence mean 2.92, shuffled 0.48, sign-flipped -0.002

## Figure 6: Prompt Journey (`fig6_prompt_journey.pdf`)

**Takeaway**: A single prompt's trajectory makes the abstract framework
concrete — from uncertain beginning through attention-steered mid-layers to
committed answer.

| Panel | Content |
|-------|---------|
| (a) | Prompt structure schematic (span blocks coloured by label) |
| (b) | delta trajectory with green/red fill + competitor identity strip |
| (c) | Cumulative component-space path (arrowed, depth-coloured) |

- **Layout**: 1x3, full-width (6.75 x 2.5 in)
- **Prompt selection**: Stable-correct prompt with highest (final_delta + 0.5 * n_competitor_switches)
- **Data files**: `decision_metrics.parquet`, `tracing_scalars.parquet`, `span_labels.parquet`
- **Tail window**: last 8 layers highlighted in yellow
